Scribamus! (Let's write!)

Now it is your time to show off your writing skills!

Don’t worry; you will get feedback to perfect your final draft.

Give it a try, and see how well you can write – in English and in Latin.

You can use the Theft at the Temple Workshop template to complete each part of this project.

Step 1:

Create and write a bulletin post about the missing Palladium. The post must include catchy taglines, a short paragraph detailing the history of the Palladium, and any current information that Detective Arcanus has gathered. You can use the sample template to help guide you to create your own bulletin post.

  • Be creative! Be sure to include images and catchy taglines to get the attention of Romans around town. You can graphically present your bulletin post in any format, as long as it resembles a flyer and contains the required information.
  • Information on the Palladium must come from properly cited research of academic sources.

Step 2:

Write three sentences in Latin, showcasing your Latin writing fluency. Include the English translations for your sentences. These sentences must relate to the crime, helping the detective with useful information or questions for his case.

  • One sentence must contain a noun-adjective pair.
  • One sentence must contain a semi-deponent verb.
  • One sentence must contain the name of one of the suspects, witnesses, or informants.

Detective Notes: Composing Sentences with Deponents

Writing sentences in English versus Latin are two very different processes! Latin word order is different from English word order. Composing sentences with subjunctive verbs is not much different than composing sentences with indicative verbs. Let this be your guide for dependent subjunctive clauses!

  1. Deponent verbs always appear with Passive voice endings. Translate deponents in the Active voice.
  2. Semi-deponent verbs appear in the Active voice in the present system and Passive voice in the perfect system. Translate semi-deponents in the Active voice.
