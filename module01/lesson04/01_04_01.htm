Missing: Priceless Palladium

A notice must immediately be posted around Rome to let all know that the Palladium has been stolen! Detective Arcanus needs you to design this "missing" flyer. Your post will be pasted on all the brick walls across Rome, so make it stand out. We need more witnesses to come forth with any information they may have. For this assignment, create a flyer detailing information about the missing Palladium.

Sample Bulletin Post:

MISSING: PALLADIUM!

Who took it?

A thief stole our precious Palladium statue from the Temple of Vesta! Without the Palladium in its resting place, Rome is doomed!

Why find it?

We want to know where the Palladium is, but first we need to know what it is. A description of its physical attributes as well as its cultural and historical importance should help the detective track it down!

What do you know?

Detective Arcanus is on the case. He has narrowed down his suspects to five individuals. He believes they all had motives to steal the Palladium. He needs to figure out motive, means, and opportunity for each suspect. So far, he has managed to interview one witness—a Vestal Priestess who was there when the crime occurred.

Any Information?

If anyone has information about the theft at the temple, please contact Detective Arcanus immediately. He can be found on the streets of Rome, interviewing people and wearing his brown cape and sandals. He’s desperate to solve this crime!

Please come forward to help solve the case. Do it for Roma (or for the sizable reward)!

Clue Commentary: Latin Sentences

Some notes to help our detective:

Sentence 1

Qualis homo Palladium mirabile ex templo Vestae corripiat?

What kind of person would steal the wondrous Palladium out of the temple of Vesta?

Sentence 2

Homines dicunt Venenatum plus laudis in terris alienis accipere solitum esse.

People are saying that Venenatus was accustomed to receiving more praise in foreign lands.

Sentence 3

Testes viderunt Venenatum Gerondamque colloquentes et gradientes extra templum in illa die.

Witnesses saw Venenatus and Geronda conversing and stepping outside the temple on that day.
