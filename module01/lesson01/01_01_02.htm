Audi et Dic: Vocabula

Adjective Focus:

  • Adjectives are either written in the 1st & 2nd Declension or in the 3rd Declension.
  • There are three types of adjectives in Latin: attributive, predicate, and substantive. Each type depends on how and where the adjective is written in the sentence. Select each tab to understand the three types of Latin adjectives.
Attributive

An attributive adjective is written right next to the noun it modifies. All attributive adjectives are written after the noun they modify except for adjectives of size or quantity, which are written before the noun. For example:

Examples Noun/Adjective Case, Number, Gender Agreement
Rex Superbus in regno habitat.
(The haughty king lives in the kingdom.)		 rex superbus—the haughty king
  • Nominative (subject)
  • Singular
  • Masculine
Is vitam bonam vivit.
(He lives a good life.)		 vitam bonam—a good life
  • Accusative (direct object)
  • Singular
  • Feminine
Pueri brevi regi cibum dederunt. brevi regi—to the short king
  • Dative
  • Singular
  • Masculine
Predicate

A predicate adjective appears in a sentence that contains a noun it modifies and a linking verb. The predicate adjective will not be written next to the noun it modifies. It will still agree with its nearest noun in case, number, and gender. For example:

Examples Noun/Adjective Case, Number, Gender Agreement
Vita est bona.
(Life is good.) 		vita bona—good life
  • Nominative (subject)
  • Singular
  • Feminine
Reges erant crudeles.
(The kings were cruel.)		 reges crudeles—cruel kings
  • Nominative (subject)
  • Plural
  • Masculine
Bella fuerant difficilia.
(The wars has been difficult.		 Bella difficilia—difficult wars
  • Nominative (subject)
  • Plural
  • Neuter
Substantive

A substantive adjective appears alone in a sentence without a noun paired with it. Translate the substantive adjective as if it is a noun. Therefore, use its gender and number to insert a "man, woman, or thing" into its translation. For example:

Examples Noun/Adjective Case, Number, Gender Agreement
Boni cibum in casam portant.
(The good men carry the food into the house.)		 boni—the good men
(Insert "men" because the adjective is masculine plural)
  • Nominative (subject)
  • Plural
  • Masculine
Dux amoenus pulchram amat.
(The pleasant leader loves the beautiful woman.) 		pulchram—beautiful woman
(Insert "woman" because the adjective is feminine singular.)
  • Accusative (direct object)
  • Singular
  • Feminine
Diu incognita spectabamus.
(For a long time, we were looking at unknown things.)		 incognita—unknown things
(Insert "things" because the adjective is Accusative plural.)
  • Accusative (direct object)
  • Plural
  • Neuter
