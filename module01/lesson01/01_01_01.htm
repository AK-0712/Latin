Sleuth Clues: Location and Stolen Object

The Palladium was a wooden statue carved in the likeness of Pallas Athena. The Romans believed that Athena herself crafted the statue. She named it the Palladium after her best friend, Pallas, whom she had accidently killed in a tragic accident. The statue held special powers and was highly coveted by those seeking ultimate supremacy. It was brought to Italy from Troy by Aeneas. In Rome, it was kept in the Temple of Vesta for centuries. Now it is missing.

Sacred Objects

The temple stored sacred objects, such as the Palladium and other objects Aeneas brought from Troy. The Temple of Vesta was chosen as the resting place of the statue because Vesta and her priestesses protected Rome from all dangers.

Roman Forum

The Roman Forum is a massive, rectangular area located in the center of Rome between the Palatine Hill and the Capitoline Hill. It was the place in Rome where Romans enjoyed gathering for social, civic, religious, and political activities. It contained magnificent structures, such the Temple of Saturn, the Temple of Castor and Pollux, the Temple of Julius Caesar, the Temple of Minerva, the Circus Maximus, and the Rostra.

Structure

The Temple of Vesta is a round temple measuring 14.8 meters in diameter. It had twenty columns over ten meters high. It was built to honor the Olympian, Vesta, goddess of the hearth. Her Vestal priestesses performed religious ceremonies there.

Vestal Priestesses

Vesta was very important to Rome’s religious institution. The Romans created the sacred Cult of Vesta, a strictly female cult of six priestesses known as Vestal Priestesses who tended to the hearth of Rome. This was the sacred fire that remained lit in the Temple of Vesta. The perennial flame embodied the life, soul, and eternal power of Rome. Because the Vestal maidens were responsible for keeping the flame lit, they were seen as the sacred lifeline of the city.
